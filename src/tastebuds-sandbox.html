Iterations

For my final iteration phase, I applied the recommendations I made from my previous usability testing. Below were my primary goals:

  • Amplify the social component.
  • Enhance visual aids.‍
  • Ensure fully understand the app's purpose by the end of onboarding.

Creating and setting up an account

Animated splash screen

Test findings

  • Users failed to comprehend the app's social features
  • Users frequently overlooked written content
  • By the end of onboarding, users struggled to remember the core premise and unique features

Iterations

  • Design an animated splash screen as a teaser
  • Add new and enhanced visual aids and microanimations for introduction carousel
  • Condense copy to under 20 words per slide
  • Enhance typography hierarchy in intro slides

Adding friends, verified influencers, and restaurant partners

I restored this step to help diners learn more about TasteBuds and its social and community-driven features. This process led to my creation of "TasteBuds influencers", which was inspired by research and testing's finding that diners also trust influencers for dining recommendations. This flow allows diners to follow friends, influencers, and restaurants.

Food preferences

Test findings

During testing, users often ignored the header copy and failed to notice the page change between ingredient preferences and allergies. To address this, I redesigned the visual and interaction elements to make these changes more noticeable and ensure users read the content. I added microanimations using transition delays and segmented the progress bar. I also enhanced visual and content clarity by refining typography, background colors, and copy.

Lastly, I removed the step to input favorite dishes, as this data can be generated over time, with diners preferring aggregate results from their friends.

  • By the end of onboarding, users struggled to remember the core premise and unique features

Searching for dining options

Home page

Test findings

  • When searching for dining options, diners begin with the home page
  • Users have the tendency to skip onboarding content
  • Users with fewer friends might see less content
  • Restaurant partnerships and influencers feature are not yet integrated into the app

Iterations

  • Place new CTAs throughout the page to introduce features and encourage engagement
  • Enhance personalization
  • Integrate restaurant partnerships into recommendation lists to boost value proposition
  • Integrate "Foodie Influencer" program into recommendation lists
  • Relocate search bar to a dedicated page in the bottom navigation bar replacing notifications to maximize content above-the-fold

Restaurant profiles

Testing found that diners want to see more details on the restaurant home page. To accomodate without compromising the above-the-fold design, I redesigned the interactive buttons to be more compact and removed the bottom navigation bar.

My iteration displays restaurant partners as a social profile, which allows diners to follow for important updates (i.e. promotions, menu items, and events). I also corrected inconsistencies in content and ensured all aggregate results have a proper data source generated from reviews.

Searching for dining options

Home page

Diners tend to explore the home page, so I rearchitected the page with new features that boost personalization and engagement. Since users tend to skip onboarding, I strategically placed new CTAs to guide them through the app. I relocated the search bar to its own dedicated page in the bottom navigation bar to maximize content above the fold (replaced Notifications). Lastly, I realized the potential issue of limited user content since they're generated exclusively by friends, so I added recommendations by influencers and new restaurant partners, aiding discovery without excess commercialization.

    Key changes

  • New CTAs and features to guide new users
  • Additional recommendation lists, featuring new restaurant partners and influencers
  • Option to reorganize and pin lists
  • Relocated 'Search' features to bottom navigation
  • Removed 'Notification' bottom navigation button

Route 3: Diner reviews a dining experience

Diners want to see additional details

I refined the visual design for consistency and removed instructional text to reduce clutter. Testing revealed that wait times and numeric price ranges are critical for diners, so I included these. I also redesigned the star rating system for a more intuitive interaction and introduced features for restaurant partners to collect premium customer insights.

    Key changes

  • Ensured design consistency
  • Removed instructional copy
  • Redesigned rating interactions
  • Added wait times and numeric price ranges
  • Enabled restaurant partners to collect diner insights
