testing and iterations

For my final iteration phase, I applied the recommendations I made from my previous usability testing. Below were my primary goals:

  • Amplify the social component.
  • Enhance visual aids.‍
  • Ensure fully understand the app's purpose by the end of onboarding.

Creating and setting up an account

Animated splash screen

Test findings

By the end of onboarding, users struggled to recall the core premise and features of TasteBuds. The most critical error was that users did not perceive TasteBuds as a social app.

Iterations

  • Design an animated splash screen as a teaser
  • Add new and enhanced visual aids and microanimations for introduction carousel
  • Condense copy to under 20 words per slide
  • Enhance typography hierarchy in intro slides

Adding friends, verified influencers, and restaurant partners

Test findings

The testing insights that informed my iterations are the same as the first slide.

Iterations

  • Restored the step to connect socials and add friends
  • Implemented a TasteBuds Influencer program
  • Added features to follow restaurants and influencers

Food preferences

Test findings

Diners often ignore instructional copy and fail to notice the page change between ingredient preferences and allergies. Some diners were confused about the relevance of inputting preferences they enjoy and worried that their selections might limit their discovery.

Iterations

  • Implement microanimations and visual cues
  • Segment progress bar with spacing
  • Enhance typography and visual hierarchy
  • Refine copy to improve content comprehension
  • Remove step to input dishes to streamline flow
  • Reorder steps to eliminate allergies first

Searching for dining options

Expanding discover page to boost engagement and clarity

Test findings

When searching for dining options, diners typically start by scrolling through the Discover page. However, many skip the onboarding content, leading to confusion about TasteBuds' features. Additionally, since content is influenced by users' social following, relying on users to add friends can result in limited user-generated content for those with fewer connections.

Iterations

  • Placed new CTAs to introduce features
  • Integrate restaurant partnerships into recommendation lists to boost value proposition
  • Highlight "Foodie Influencers" on Discover page
  • Update bottom navigation with a search page
  • Remove search bar to prioritize content visibility

Iteration

Diners want to easily access restaurant details and updates

Test findings

Diners typically scroll through the restaurant home page before exploring other sections, so they seek more detailed information upfront. While restaurant promotions strongly incentivize diners, they often miss these deals if they don't actively follow the restaurants on social media.

Iterations

  • Expand details on the restaurant home page
  • Strengthen social presence with new dedicated 'Posts' and 'Events' pages
  • Redesign to resemble social profiles with CTAs
  • Remove bottom navigation to prioritize content
  • Make interactive buttons more compact
  • Enhance visual design for better readability

Home page

Diners tend to explore the home page, so I rearchitected the page with new features that boost personalization and engagement. Since users tend to skip onboarding, I strategically placed new CTAs to guide them through the app. I relocated the search bar to its own dedicated page in the bottom navigation bar to maximize content above the fold (replaced Notifications). Lastly, I realized the potential issue of limited user content since they're generated exclusively by friends, so I added recommendations by influencers and new restaurant partners, aiding discovery without excess commercialization.

    Key changes

  • New CTAs and features to guide new users
  • Additional recommendation lists, featuring new restaurant partners and influencers
  • Option to reorganize and pin lists
  • Relocated 'Search' features to bottom navigation
  • Removed 'Notification' bottom navigation button
