Iterations

For my final iteration phase, I applied the recommendations I made from my previous usability testing. Below were my primary goals:

  • Amplify the social component.
  • Enhance visual aids.‍
  • Ensure fully understand the app's purpose by the end of onboarding.

Creating and setting up an account

Animated splash screen

My iterations here focused on content strategy and visual design. To address confusion with the premise of TasteBuds, my first solution was an animated splash screen that serves as an informative teaser. It simulates real-time user notifications for an immersive experience.

Crafting universally relevant copy was a big challenge. In the end, I decided to fuse ambiguity and specificity by crafting copy like @ThatRestaurantYouHaven'tTried and Your Best Friend Recommends.

Introduction carousel

My second solution to clarify the premise of TasteBuds was a revamped introduction carousel. I designed new visual aids that continue my content strategy for the splash screen, showing a glimpse of the in-app experience. I also shortened my copy to under 20 words per slide and implemented conversational UI.

Adding friends, verified influencers, and restaurant partners

I restored this step to help diners learn more about TasteBuds and its social and community-driven features. This process led to my creation of "TasteBuds influencers", which was inspired by research and testing's finding that diners also trust influencers for dining recommendations. This flow allows diners to follow friends, influencers, and restaurants.

Food preferences

During testing, users often ignored the header copy and failed to notice the page change between ingredient preferences and allergies. To address this, I redesigned the visual and interaction elements to make these changes more noticeable and ensure users read the content. I added microanimations using transition delays and segmented the progress bar. I also enhanced visual and content clarity by refining typography, background colors, and copy.

Lastly, I removed the step to input favorite dishes, as this data can be generated over time, with diners preferring aggregate results from their friends.

    Key changes

  • Reordered steps to eliminate allergies first
  • Removed yellow background for visual clarity
  • Added a "Save & finish later" button
  • Color coded descriptive heading
  • Added microanimations to and enhanced visual feedback

Searching for dining options

Home page

When searching for dining options, the first thing diners do is scroll through the Discover page, so I designed additional features to create a more personalized and immersive experience. To address the tendency of users skipping onboarding, I strategically placed new CTAs throughout the page to guide them. I relocated the search bar to a dedicated page in the bottom navigation bar, replacing Notifications, to maximize content visibility above the fold. I also realized there is a potential issue of limited user-generated content since they depend on the user's following, so I incorporated recommendations from influencers and new restaurant partners.

    Key changes

  • New CTAs and features to guide new users
  • Additional recommendation lists, featuring new restaurant partners and influencers
  • Option to reorganize and pin lists
  • Relocated 'Search' features to bottom navigation
  • Removed 'Notification' bottom navigation button

Restaurant listing

Testing found that diners want to see more details on the restaurant home page. To provide more details without compromising the above-the-fold design, I redesigned the interactive buttons to be more compact and removed the bottom navigation bar.

I revamped the page to resemble a social profile, allowing diners to follow restaurant partners for updates on promotions, new menu items, and events.

Lastly, I corrected inconsistencies in content and ensured all aggregate results have a proper data source generated from reviews.

    Key changes

  • Content

Discover page

Route 3: Diner reviews a dining experience

Diners want to see additional details

I refined the visual design for consistency and removed instructional text to reduce clutter. Testing revealed that wait times and numeric price ranges are critical for diners, so I included these. I also redesigned the star rating system for a more intuitive interaction and introduced features for restaurant partners to collect premium customer insights.

    Key changes

  • Ensured design consistency
  • Removed instructional copy
  • Redesigned rating interactions
  • Added wait times and numeric price ranges
  • Enabled restaurant partners to collect diner insights
