High-fidelity prototype

With my new style guide, I designed high-fidelity wireframes and prototyped them for my next round of usability tests. A key objective in my high-fidelity explorations was enhancing accessibility, so I reviewed Apple's Human Interface Guidelines for Accessibility and Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG).

My learnings guided me to significantly increase spacing and physical interactions when possible, supporting both users with limited mobility and power users.

This reinforced my dedication to accessibility, one of my core design values!

Route 1: Sign up + onboarding

Given how essential food preferences are to curate accurate dining recommendations, my goal was to make onboarding more engaging to encourage users to input preferences now than later. Therefore, I implemented more conversational UI and visual aids and further streamlined the user flow.

Route 2: Diner searches for a restaurant

For this route, my design considerations included accessibility, functionality, and business goals. I focused on fixing my mistake of overcrowding screens in my low-fidelity wireframes, so I not only drew from my test insights but also revisited diner personas to balance essential information and accessibility. Revisiting the personas helped my struggle with striking a balance between diners' mental models and innovative features that define my unique value proposition! Lastly, I began conceptualizing integrating restaurant partnerships into the diner journey.

This was a really important lesson for me! I'll discuss this further in my reflection.

For You

I focused on enhancing engagement and accessibility while emphasizing a personalized user experience. Therefore, I reorganized the layout to improve the information and content hierarchy, redesigned the navigation tabs to be more distinct, revised the label and navigational copy, and implemented guiding text in the search bar using conversational UI.

Following

Using the low-fidelity review page in restaurant listings, I designed the "Following" page. The most challenging part of this process was trying to balance essential information and social engagement while respecting user mental models.

My final design reorganizes the content hierarchy and simplifies the layout. Diners may engage with reviews posted by their friends in a few ways: They may (1) react with any emoji, (2) leave comments, (3) save the review for future reference, and (4) navigate to the reviewed restaurant and menu items.
